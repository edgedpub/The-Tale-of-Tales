May a thousand ills befall thee! May the seed of it be scattered, thou scoundrel, thou rogue, thou son of a caged woman, thou rogue!”

The boy, who had little hair on his cheeks and less discretion, feeling that this intemperate insult had fallen on him, repaid her in kind: “Don’t you want to bind up this shithole, you Parasacian hag, bloodsucking witch, child-sucking, fart-face? The old woman, on hearing this news from her house, became so furious that, losing the compass of phlegm and escaping from the stable of patience, she lifted the curtain of the apparatus and showed the woodland scene, in which Silvio could say: “Go and open eyes with your horn”. At the sight of which Zoza was seized with such a fit of laughter that she almost broke down.

The old woman, at the sound of this mockery, became angry, and, turning to Zoza with a scowl, “Go! - she said to her - may you not find a shadow of a husband if you do not take the prince of Round Field!”. The princess, hearing these words, summoned her, and wanted to know for every account whether he had meant to speak insult to her or cast a blasphemy upon her. And the old woman answered her:

“Know that the prince I have named is a graceful person and his name is Tadeo, who by the imprecation of a fairy has given the last touch to the picture of life and has been placed in a tomb outside the city walls. On that tomb is an inscription that says that any woman who in three days will fill an amphora, which can be seen hanging there on a hook, with tears will resurrect him and take him for a husband. But it is impossible that two human eyes can weep so many drops as to fill an amphora that contains half a stadium (if it were not, as I have heard told, that Egeria, who made herself a fountain of tears in Rome); and therefore I, seeing myself mocked and scorned by you, have given you this blasphemy, and I pray Heaven that it may succeed in piety, in revenge for the insult done to me”. Having said this, she walked down the steps, fearful of some beating.
