You see, because of this desire, artisans leave their foundations, merchants leave their shops, doctors leave their cases, and shopkeepers leave their affairs, and go open-mouthed through barbers’ shops and gossiping circles to hear false tidings, invented notices and gazettes in the air. I must therefore excuse my wife for putting herself in this melancholic mood of listening to fairy tales. And if it pleases you to give my princess’s desire a pitcher and to strike at the heart of my cravings, you will be glad, for four or five days while her belly is still late in deflating, to tell each of you every day a fairy tale of the kind that old women are wont to tell to entertain children. You will always find yourselves at the same place, where first we will eat, then we will begin the chatter, and the day will be ended by some eclogue that will be recited by our own belly-swallowers; and so we will cheerfully pass our lives, and sad who dies!”

At these words they all accepted Tadeo’s command with a nod of their heads; and meanwhile, the tables having been set and the food having arrived, they began to eat, and, when they had finished gorging, the prince beckoned to Zeza, who was slovenly, to set fire to the piece. Zeza, having made a great bow to the prince and his wife, thus began to speak.
