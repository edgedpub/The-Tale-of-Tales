And, begging her greatly to forgive her impertinence to the desires of a pregnant woman, Zoza, who was tickled to death in the presence of the cause of her travails, forced herself and allowed herself to be begged and begged to hold back the rowing of the boat and enjoy the sight of her lord more time, stolen from him by an ugly slave. In the end, giving him the doll as she had done with other things, before handing it over to him, she blew to that little figure who had put in the slave girl’s breast the desire to hear fairy tales.

Tadeo, who discovered himself with the doll in hand, without shelling out a penny, was astonished at such courtesy, offered her state and life in exchange for that favour and, returning to the palace, handed the doll to his wife. The slave girl put her in her lap to amuse herself; and lo, she immediately seemed to be Love in the form of Ascanius in Dido’s lap, who lit a fire in her breast; for so hot a desire arose in the slave girl to hear fairy tales that, not being able to resist, and doubting to touch her mouth and have children so querulous as to annoy a whole ship of beggars, she called her husband as usual and repeated to him “If you don’t come people and count fairy tales, you’ll me punch belly and little Giorgie kill!”.

Tadeo, in order to remove this nuisance, ordered a proclamation that all the women of the country should come to him on a given day. And on that day, at the rising of the star Diana, who wakes the Dawn to adorn the streets through which the Sun must pass, they all came to the appointed place. But as he did not like to keep all this riffraff out of the way because of his wife’s particular taste, as well as the fact that it was suffocating to see such a crowd, he chose only ten of them, the best in the city, who seemed to him the quickest and most chatty; and they were lame Zeza, twisted Cecca, goitered Meneca, big-nosed Tolla, hunch-back Popa, drooling Antonella, snout-faced Ciulla, cross-eyed Paola, mangy Ciommetella, and shitty.

And, having written these names on a card and dismissed the others, he and the slave girl got up from under the canopy and walked with measured steps to a garden in the same palace, where the leafy branches were so tangled that the Sun with the pole of rays could not divide them.
