Antuono, who saw that the thing had succeeded to the point and the macaroni had fallen into the cheese and the broccoli into the lard, said: ‘There is no remedy! You will die of blows if you do not return my things to me’.

And the innkeeper, who was beaten to a pulp, shouted: “Take all that I have, but get this cursed nuisance off my back!”; and, to give Antuono security, he made him come first with what he had taken from him. Antuono, when he had everything in his hands, said, “Lie down, mace!”-, and that one crouched down stretched out on one side. So he took the donkey and the other things, and went to his mother’s house, where, having made a royal test of the donkey’s ass and a sure test of the napkin, he collected a great deal of money, married his sisters, enriched his mother, and proved the truth of the saying: God helps fools and children.”
