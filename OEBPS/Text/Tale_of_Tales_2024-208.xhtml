VThe Dragon

Fifth Entertainment of the Fourth Day

Due to the actions of a queen, Miuccio is sent off to face various dangers, and he manages to overcome all of them honorably with the help of an enchanted bird. The queen finally dies, and when it is discovered that Miuccio is the king’s son he frees his mother, who becomes the wife of that king.

The tale of the seven little pork rinds fattened up the soup of the prince’s pleasure to such a degree that it was dripping with oil as he tasted the ignorant malice and the malicious ignorance of Saporita, which had been ladled out with so much gusto by Tolla. But Popa, not wanting to do a crumb worse than Tolla, sailed off on the sea of nursery stories with the tale that follows: He who seeks the injury of another finds his own hurt; and he who spreads the snares of treachery and deceit often falls into them himself; as you shall hear in the story of a queen, who with her own hands constructed the trap in which she was caught by the foot.
