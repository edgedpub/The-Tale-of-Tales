By chance, the slave girl saw and heard him, and became so enamoured of him that, calling Tadeo, she said to him: “If you don’t have that little fellow to sing, me punch belly and little Giorgie kill!”. The prince, who had had his bard put on by Bernaguallà, immediately sent to ask Zoza if she would sell it to him; and Zoza replied that she was no merchant, but that if she would accept it as a gift, she would take it, for she would gladly make it known to him. Tadeo, who was always at pains to keep his wife contained so that she would give birth, accepted the offer.

Four days later, Zoza opened the chestnut and out of it came a hen with twelve golden chicks, which, placed above the same window and seen by the slave girl, pierced her with a most acute desire; whereupon, calling Tadeo and pointing out to him that thing so beautiful, she said to him: “If that hen does not catch, me punch belly and little Giorgie kill!”

And Tadeo, who allowed himself to be intimidated and dominated by this bitch, sent again to Zoza to offer her what she liked to ask for the price of such a beautiful hen. And he got the same answer as last time, that he might as well have taken her as a gift, for, to deal in terms of buying and selling, it would have been words in the wind. And he, who could do no less, let necessity drive out discretion, and, taking away this beautiful morsel, was astonished at the liberality of a female, a sex so greedy by nature that all the golden rods from the Indies would not suffice.

As many days went by, Zoza opened the hazelnut, from which came out a doll spinning gold, which was truly a thing to behold, and as soon as it was placed in the same window and caught the slave girl’s eye, she called Tadeo again and repeated the usual tune: “If you don’t buy a doll, me punch belly and little Giorgie kill “.
